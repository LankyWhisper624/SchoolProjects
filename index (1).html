Glacier Peak Volcano

Location:


Glacier Peak is located only 70 miles away from Seattle.

History:


13000 years ago, Glacier Peak erupted very violently. The laharswere so large that they reached the sea.

Type and information

Glacier Peakis a composite volcano. It has over a dozen glaciers, fresh looking domes of dacite, and three hot springs.

It has explosive eruptions that spew out hot gas and debris. Its last massive eruption was in the 900's (CE). The last time it erupted was in the 1700's. This makes Glacier Peak an active volcano.

it is currently 10,541 feet high (3212.9 meters). Glacier Peak and Mt. St. Helens are the only volcanoes in Washington State to have had a massive eruption in the past 15,000 years.

    Interesting things about the volcano:
  • It has three summits: two false summits and one real summit.
  • it has camping, hiking, and flora, including the Showy Jacob's ladder and the Tiger Lily.
  • The Native American name is "Takobia". It first appeared with its current name in 1898.
Fast Facts:
  • It erupted in six separate episodes.
  • It is the most remote of the five active volcanoes in Washington State.
  • Its big eryption in the 900's is comparable to Mt. St. Helens' eruption in 1980.
  • It is overlooked bu the general population because of its obscurity from its location and hight.
  • The original cone's age is unknown due to erosion from glaciers and eruptions from younger cones.
